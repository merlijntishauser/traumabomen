# Voiceover Script

Read at a warm, professional pace. Emphasize key phrases in **bold**.

## Scene 1: Landing (0:00 - 0:05)

Welcome to Traumabomen, a personal reflection tool for mapping **intergenerational trauma** onto visual family trees.

[PAUSE 2s]

## Scene 2: Registration (0:05 - 0:30)

Let's create an account. You'll notice we ask for two separate credentials: a **password** for authentication, and an **encryption passphrase** that protects your data.

[PAUSE 2s]

The password strength meter gives real-time feedback as you type.

[PAUSE 1s]

The encryption passphrase is critical. It never leaves your device. Your sensitive data is encrypted **before** it reaches our servers, meaning we can never read it. If you lose this passphrase, your data cannot be recovered.

[PAUSE 2s]

## Scene 3: Empty Trees (0:30 - 0:35)

After registration, you arrive at your tree list. Right now it's empty. Let's fix that.

[PAUSE 1s]

## Scene 4: Create Demo Tree (0:35 - 0:43)

We'll use the **demo tree** to explore the app. This creates a fictional family, the Whitfields, with pre-populated relationships, trauma events, and life events.

[PAUSE 2s]

## Scene 5: Canvas Exploration (0:43 - 1:03)

Here's the family tree canvas. Each person is shown as a **node**, with lines connecting family relationships. Solid lines represent biological connections; dashed lines show step or adoptive relationships.

[PAUSE 2s]

The small **badges** on each person tell a story at a glance: circles for trauma events, squares for life events, and triangles for clinical classifications. Each is color-coded by category.

[PAUSE 2s]

You can pan and zoom the canvas freely to explore the family structure.

[PAUSE 3s]

## Scene 6: Person Detail (1:03 - 1:18)

Clicking a person opens their **detail panel**. Here you can see Dorothy Whitfield's information, including birth and death years.

[PAUSE 2s]

The **Events** tab shows trauma events, life events, and turning points. The **DSM-5** tab tracks suspected or diagnosed classifications with time periods.

[PAUSE 3s]

## Scene 7: Add Person (1:18 - 1:33)

Adding a new family member is straightforward. Click the plus button, fill in a name and birth year, and save.

[PAUSE 2s]

We're adding Emma Whitfield, born 2010, the next generation of this family.

[PAUSE 3s]

## Scene 8: Create Relationship (1:33 - 1:43)

After adding a person, the app prompts you to **connect** them to an existing family member. Select the parent and the relationship type. The connection appears immediately on the canvas.

[PAUSE 3s]

## Scene 9: Timeline View (1:43 - 1:58)

The **timeline** arranges the family chronologically. Each person's lifespan appears as a horizontal bar, with trauma events and life events marked along it.

[PAUSE 2s]

This view is powerful for spotting **patterns across generations**: when did key events cluster? What years were hardest?

[PAUSE 3s]

## Scene 10: Patterns View (1:58 - 2:08)

The **Patterns** page lets you explicitly link events across people and generations. You can name a pattern, assign a color, and connect the trauma events, life events, or classifications that form the thread.

[PAUSE 3s]

## Scene 11: Journal (2:08 - 2:18)

The **journal** is your personal reflection space. Write entries about what you're discovering. You can link entries to specific **turning points** in a person's life.

[PAUSE 3s]

## Scene 12: Insights (2:18 - 2:23)

The **insights** page summarizes patterns the app has detected: generational trends, temporal clusters, and resilience indicators.

[PAUSE 2s]

## Scene 13: Theme Switching (2:23 - 2:35)

Traumabomen offers multiple visual themes. The **light theme** uses warm linen and sage tones. The **watercolor theme** brings soft teal-blue on parchment, inspired by hand-painted journals.

[PAUSE 2s]

Each theme is carefully designed to feel **calm and organic**, respecting the sensitive nature of this work.

[PAUSE 2s]

## Scene 14: Logout and Re-login (2:35 - 2:50)

Finally, let's verify **persistence**. After logging out and back in, you'll see the familiar unlock screen. Enter your encryption passphrase, and your family tree reappears exactly as you left it.

[PAUSE 2s]

All data was decrypted locally on your device. Our servers only stored encrypted blobs they cannot read.

[PAUSE 2s]

Thank you for watching. Traumabomen is a tool for personal reflection, helping you understand how family experiences echo across generations.
